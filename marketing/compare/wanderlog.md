# The honest case for Wanderlog

We're going to spend the bulk of this page explaining why VenturePath beats Wanderlog for Squads. But before we do, here's what Wanderlog gets right:

- **Free tier is genuinely free.** Unlimited stops, unlimited collaborators, an actual map. No nag screens.
- **Real-time multi-cursor editing is the bar.** When two Pioneers edit at once, you see their cursor. That feels like Google Docs because that's what it copied, and it copied it well.
- **Route optimization with travel times** between stops is fast and trustworthy.
- **The mobile apps are mature.** iOS and Android, both polished. VenturePath's mobile experience is the web app today; native apps are on our roadmap.
- **Gmail import on Pro is fast.** Forward a flight confirmation, it appears.

If you're a solo Pioneer planning a budget trip and you don't need Tactical Mode or Squad voting, Wanderlog is probably the right call. We mean it.

# Where VenturePath wins #1: **Squad voting** (the real gap)

The honest weakness of every shared-doc planner — Wanderlog, Mindtrip, Roadtrippers — is that *editing* is not the same as *deciding*.

When four Pioneers can all edit at once, decisions still happen in chat: someone proposes a restaurant, two people thumbs-up in chat, one person disagrees, the proposal sits, nobody knows if it's settled, and someone quietly removes it three days later. The chat scrolls. The veto disappears.

**The Ledger Workbench** is VenturePath's answer:

1. **Nominated** — any Pioneer drops a candidate (restaurant, stop, excursion) into the first column.
2. **In Vote** — Pioneers drag their avatar onto + or − targets. The live vote bar updates as squad-mates weigh in.
3. **Settled or Vetoed** — passed votes roll into logistics with auto-pending settlements; vetoed items get a "torn paper" rejection animation that ends the debate.

Now your Squad has a *legible record* of what was decided, by whom, and when — without anyone having to scroll the chat. For Squads of 3+ with at least one strong-opinioned Pioneer, this single feature changes the success rate of group planning.

Wanderlog has shared editing. We have shared deciding.

# Where VenturePath wins #2: **Tactical Mode**

Wanderlog Pro offers offline access — you can view your trip without signal. That's good. It is also *the floor*, not the ceiling.

**Tactical Mode** is a single Tactical-Amber-on-near-black surface designed for when the rest of the internet stops working:

- **Offline cache of your full Expedition** — every Leg, every confirmation, every map tile, on every tier.
- **what3words SOS** — the beacon generates a pre-composed emergency message including your what3words address (the format [85% of UK emergency services use](https://what3words.com/news/emergency/uk-emergency-services-and-the-aa-recommend-downloading-what3words)), lat/lng, expedition name, and last-known Squad roster — copy-able with one tap.
- **CompassRing** — live GPS bearing to the next stop without needing map tiles.
- **Itinerary-anchored alerts** — disruption warnings keyed to your exact Leg, not a generic country-level advisory.
- **Neighborhood safety overlay** — color-coded safety scores including women's, LGBTQ+, day-vs-night, where supported.

Tactical Mode isn't a feature you use every day. It's the feature that keeps your Squad whole when something goes sideways — a delayed flight, a missed transfer, a lost phone in a country you don't speak. Wanderlog's offline view is a cached web page. Tactical Mode is a survival surface.

# Where VenturePath wins #3: **Squad packing weight-share**

Wanderlog has a packing list. So does TripIt. So does PackPoint. They are all solo lists.

**VentureVault's PackingManifest** treats your Squad as one shared load:

- A horizontal Pioneer strip across the top shows avatars and carried weight in JetBrains Mono kilograms.
- Drag a tent from your bag to a squad-mate's bag and weight rebalances live; their Bag3D animates the new fill level.
- Climate-driven Ember "Scout Recommends" chips surface from the trip's weather data.
- Overload past 20% of body weight triggers a Tactical-Amber warning band — you cannot ignore an unsafe load.

Lighterpack tracks weight beautifully but solo. PackPoint suggests activity-aware packing but solo. **Nobody does Squad redistribution.** For trips where the bag matters — outdoor, multi-day hiking, family travel where parents end up carrying their kids' stuff — this is the difference between "we packed a list" and "we packed an Expedition."

(Solo Pioneers see the same UI with only their own avatar. No template-grid feeling.)

# Where VenturePath wins #4: **Pro-Paths and post-trip**

Wanderlog's marketplace shows "Travel Plans" you can browse and copy. That's a fine read-only library. The economics flow one way.

**VentureVault Pro-Paths are forkable with creator revenue share.** An Architect publishes a 10-day Lisbon Expedition. A Pioneer clones it, modifies it, runs their version. The original Architect earns revenue share on the clone — not a one-time guide sale.

- **Public Architect earnings** on a leaderboard at `/architects` (the Substack/OnlyFans transparency model).
- **Creator-AI trained on each Architect's voice** (P1 — the Rexby pattern, defensible per-Architect moat).
- **Auto-convert finished Expeditions into Pro-Path drafts** — the AfterActionScreen's last step prompts you to publish.

And then there's the loop nobody else closes: **after-trip Trip Reel.** When an Expedition ends, AfterActionScreen auto-generates a 60–90 second cinematic recap from your Squad's photo strip and the RouteMap path-trace, with Playfair title cards and optional Tactical-Amber stat callouts. The Reel ends with a one-tap CTA to publish the Expedition as a Pro-Path — every memory becomes the next Pioneer's plan, and the Architect keeps earning.

Wanderlog doesn't generate post-trip content. Polarsteps does, but Polarsteps doesn't plan. **VenturePath is the only product that closes the plan → survive → share → earn loop.**

# Frequently asked questions

**Can I import my Wanderlog trips into VenturePath?**
Direct Wanderlog import is on our roadmap. The fastest path today: forward your booking confirmations to `plans@venturepath.com` and the parser auto-builds an Expedition with your flights, hotels, and reservations.

**Is VenturePath free?**
Yes — the Free Architect tier includes one active Expedition with unlimited Squad collaborators, full VentureVault browsing, and basic Tactical Mode. Pioneer Pass ($5.99/mo · $39/yr) unlocks unlimited Expeditions, full offline, and Trip Reel export.

**Does Wanderlog have group voting?**
Not as a structured feature. Wanderlog has real-time multi-cursor editing on a shared trip; decisions still happen in their chat or comments. VenturePath's Ledger Workbench is a structured nominate-vote-veto board.

**Is Wanderlog's offline mode as good as Tactical Mode?**
Wanderlog Pro caches your trip for offline viewing — that's a cached web page. Tactical Mode includes offline cache *plus* what3words SOS generation, GPS CompassRing, itinerary-anchored alerts, and neighborhood safety overlays in one Tactical-Amber emergency surface.

**Which has better mobile apps?**
Wanderlog today. VenturePath is a polished mobile-web experience now, with native iOS/Android apps on our roadmap (Capacitor or Expo build).

**Can I use both?**
You can. Many Pioneers use Wanderlog for solo budget trips and VenturePath for squad Expeditions. They're not mutually exclusive — they're built for different jobs.
